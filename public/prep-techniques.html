Coffee is a beverage brewed from roasted coffee beans. Darkly colored, bitter, and slightly acidic, coffee has a stimulating effect on humans, primarily due to its caffeine content.

The two most commonly grown coffee bean types are C. arabica and C. robusta. Coffee plants are cultivated in over 70 countries, primarily in the equatorial regions of the Americas, Southeast Asia, the Indian subcontinent, and Africa.

Brazil, Vietnam, and Colombia are the top exporters of coffee beans as of 2023. The word coffee entered the English language in 1582 via the Dutch koffie, borrowed from the Ottoman Turkish kahve, borrowed in turn from the Arabic qahwah.

Coffee is usually sold in a roasted state, and with rare exceptions, such as infusions from green coffee beans, coffee is roasted before it is consumed. The roasting process influences the taste of the beverage by changing the coffee bean both physically and chemically.

There are many types of coffee, including Turkish Ibrik coffee, also known as Turkish coffee, which is a traditional method of brewing coffee using finely ground coffee beans in a small pot called an Ibrik or cezve.
